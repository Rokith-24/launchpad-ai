⚡ The 5 Prompts
Digital Vault — Free Starter Resource

From zero idea to ready to sell — in one day

Use these prompts in order. Each one moves you one step closer to your first sale. Replace anything in [brackets] with your own details.

1
Find Your Niche
Use when → you don't know what to sell
Copy this prompt →
I want to sell a digital product but I'm not sure what niche to pick. Ask me 5 quick questions about my interests, skills, and problems I've personally solved. Then suggest 3 beginner-friendly digital product niches I can realistically enter in the next 30 days — with a one-line reason for each and a description of who the buyer would be.
💡 Answer the AI's questions honestly. The more specific your answers, the more targeted and profitable the niche suggestions will be.
2
Validate Demand Before You Build
Use when → before creating anything
Copy this prompt →
I'm thinking about creating a [ebook / checklist / template] about [your topic] for [your audience]. Without spending any money, give me 5 free ways to test if people actually want to buy this before I create it. For each method, tell me what a positive signal looks like vs a negative one, and how long it should take to test.
💡 Skip this step and you risk spending days building something nobody wants. Take 10 minutes to validate — it changes everything.
3
Create Your Product Outline
Use when → your idea is validated and you're ready to build
Copy this prompt →
Act as an expert digital product creator. I want to make a [ebook / checklist / template / guide] about [your topic] for [your audience]. Create a complete outline with: a compelling title, 5–7 sections with 3 sub-points each, and one clear actionable takeaway per section. Make it feel premium and genuinely worth paying for.
💡 Use this outline directly as your table of contents. Each section becomes one chapter or page of your finished product.

4
Write an Instagram Caption That Sells
Use when → your product is ready and you need launch content
Copy this prompt →
Write an Instagram caption to sell my digital product: [product name] about [topic], priced at [price]. The caption must: open with a scroll-stopping hook (no questions as the first line), describe 1 painful problem the reader has right now, show how my product solves it, list 3 bullet-point benefits, and end with a bold CTA. Keep it under 200 words. Give me 3 different versions with different hooks.
💡 Post all 3 versions across 3 consecutive days and track which one gets the most saves and DMs — that's your winning formula to repeat.
5
Price It Right
Use when → you're deciding what to charge
Copy this prompt →
I've created a [type of product] about [topic] targeting [your audience] in India. Help me price it correctly. Consider: the results it helps buyers get, what similar products sell for, the Indian digital product market, and beginner buyer psychology. Give me 3 pricing options — budget, standard and premium — with a one-line justification for each. Tell me which you'd recommend and why.
💡 Don't underprice out of fear. If your product saves someone 10 hours of research, it's worth charging for that time.
